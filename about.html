About Me

I started out as a visual artist, sketching concept worlds for a daring startup and freelancing to keep the lights on after it folded. That season trained my eye for detail and taught me how to keep creating even when the brief changes mid-stroke.

From there I moved into sales at Bearing Man Group, where I learned how to read people, frame technical value, and stay calm when the stakes were real. Somewhere between late-night proposals and early morning client calls I fell for code—it let me build solutions with the same creative energy, but with outcomes you can deploy.

Today I ship backend services and data pipelines, still carrying that artist's curiosity. The mission is to blend creative tech with operational reliability—whether it's a CMS rollout or an Airflow DAG that keeps campaign data fresh. I love leading from the trenches, pairing junior developers, and translating complex systems into stories teams can rally behind.

What I Do

I specialize in building scalable backend systems using .NET and Python. My work spans from real-time multiplayer applications to data engineering pipelines. I'm passionate about clean architecture, test-driven development, and creating systems that teams can understand and maintain.

Currently, I'm building marketing automation services at Ogilvy South Africa, where I develop RESTful APIs in C# and ASP.NET Core that power campaign personalization and CRM integrations.

Technical Philosophy

I believe in writing code that tells a story. Every class, every function, every variable name should communicate intent. I favor composition over inheritance, simplicity over cleverness, and tests over documentation (though good documentation is still essential).

My approach combines the rigor of traditional software engineering with the pragmatism needed to ship products. I'm equally comfortable discussing SOLID principles and database normalization as I am pair-programming through a gnarly bug at 2 AM.
